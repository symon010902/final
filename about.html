Program Description:

The Bachelor in Automotive Technology (BAT) was designed as a ladderized curriculum to train learners to become automotive engineering technologists, industrial technicians, and entrepreneurs for supervisory and managerial positions to cater to the needs of automotive industries globally and to generate employment n the field of automotive technology.

Program Outcomes:

The graduates of the BAT program should be able to:

  1. Use techniques, skills, and modern technological tools necessary for professional practice;
  2. Keep abreast with the developments of technology;
  3. Carry out managerial and supervisory tasks;
  4. Communicate effectively to the society the various technological projects and the benefits that can be derived from it;
  5. Practice the profession ethically and responsibly;
  6. Identify contemporary issues and their implications to society;
  7. Act as a member or team leader to manage projects in multidisciplinary environments by applying management principles; and
  8. Pursue lifelong learning for personal and professional growth through varied experimental and field-based opportunities.
